Common Questions

Understanding Natural Remedies & Supplements

Honest answers to help you choose wisely, combine thoughtfully, and know when to seek professional guidance.

How do I choose the right natural remedy for my needs?

Start by identifying your primary concern — whether it's sleep quality, digestive comfort, energy, or immunity. Look for remedies with a clear tradition of use and, where possible, clinical evidence behind the key ingredient. At Valesų gerovė, every product in our selection is chosen for transparency of sourcing and documented purpose, so you can make an informed decision rather than a speculative one.

Can I combine multiple supplements at the same time?

Many supplements work well together — for example, magnesium glycinate for sleep pairs naturally with calming botanicals like lemon balm or passionflower. However, some combinations can compete for absorption or amplify effects unexpectedly. As a general rule, introduce one new supplement at a time, observe your response over two to four weeks, and consult a healthcare professional if you take any prescription medications.

What does "natural" actually mean on a product label?

"Natural" is not a regulated term in most markets, so it requires scrutiny. Look for specific certifications — organic, non-GMO, or third-party tested — and read the full ingredient list. At Valesų gerovė, we prioritise products free from unnecessary fillers, artificial binders, and synthetic colourants, and we favour suppliers who publish their sourcing and testing documentation openly.

How long before I notice results from a new supplement?

This depends greatly on the supplement and the individual. Adaptogenic herbs like ashwagandha or rhodiola typically require four to eight weeks of consistent use before noticeable effects emerge. Minerals such as magnesium glycinate may support sleep quality within days. Fat-soluble vitamins like D3 build up over weeks. Patience and consistency are the most reliable predictors of a positive outcome.

How do I assess the quality of a supplement brand?

Quality indicators include third-party testing (look for certificates of analysis), GMP-certified manufacturing facilities, clear disclosure of ingredient origins, and standardised extract ratios where applicable. Avoid brands that make exaggerated health claims without substantiation. A trustworthy brand in the natural medicine space will be transparent about what their product contains — and what it does not.

Are natural remedies safe during pregnancy or breastfeeding?

Not all natural products are safe during pregnancy or lactation. Some herbs — including certain adaptogens and high-dose botanicals — are contraindicated during these periods. Always consult your midwife, obstetrician, or GP before starting any supplement while pregnant or breastfeeding. This is a non-negotiable step, regardless of how gentle or plant-based a product appears.

When should I seek professional medical advice instead?

Natural remedies and dietary supplements are supportive tools — they are not substitutes for medical diagnosis or treatment. Seek professional advice if you experience persistent or worsening symptoms, if you are managing a chronic condition, or if you are unsure whether a supplement interacts with your current medications. Valesų gerovė encourages a collaborative approach: natural support alongside, not instead of, qualified medical care.

Do you carry supplements for specific goals like muscle recovery?

Yes — our range includes targeted nutritional support for active individuals, including plant-based protein options and recovery-focused formulas. Whether you are exploring protein shakes for muscle gain or seeking mineral support for post-exercise recovery, our selection focuses on clean formulations with minimal additives. We are happy to guide you toward products aligned with your specific goals and lifestyle.
